Core Ideas

Three concepts, one operating logic

Small reasoning errors don't stay small. They amplify through every layer of a decision chain — in supply chains, in teams, in AI. These three frameworks map the distortion, measure the cognition, and design the collaboration.

Cognitive Bullwhip Effect

In supply chain, the Bullwhip Effect turns a 5% demand dip into a 40% production cut. Each node amplifies the original distortion.

AI reasoning does the same thing. A small misread compounds through framing, analysis, and recommendation — producing a confidently wrong output from a minor input error.

→ Demand forecast off by 5%
→ Safety stock recalculated without root cause
→ Procurement over-orders by 30%
→ Warehouse overflow, emergency markdowns
→ 5% signal. 6-figure cost.

Structured Cognition Model

Most diagnostic tools measure what you know. This one measures how you think — the structural quality of reasoning itself.

Six levels of cognitive maturity. At Level 1, you react to symptoms. At Level 6, you design the system that prevents symptoms from occurring. The gap between each level isn't knowledge — it's the ability to see one layer deeper into causation.

Hybrid Mind

Cognitive Bullwhip maps where reasoning breaks. Structured Cognition measures how well you think. Hybrid Mind answers the next question: how should humans and AI think together?

It's a 3-layer cognitive operating model — separating what AI should handle (speed, recall, pattern matching) from what humans must own (judgment, framing, ethical weight). The goal isn't AI augmentation. It's designing the interface between two kinds of intelligence so neither degrades the other.

Layer 1
AI Engine — speed, recall, pattern
Layer 2
Interface — validation, override, context
Layer 3
Human Core — judgment, framing, ethics
